The  algorithm  should  operate  in  seven  stages.  
1.  Normalize  and  fingerprint  every  source

Every  source  must  be  converted  into  a  canonical  internal  form.  
Each  source  gets:  
●  source_id  ●  source_type  ●  author  or  origin  ●  publication  date  ●  acquisition  date  ●  checksum  /  hash  ●  extraction  method  ●  jurisdiction  or  domain  ●  revision/version  id

●  an  original  filing  from  a  repost  ●  a  primary  witness  from  a  commentary  article  ●  an  archive  scan  from  a  blog  summary

6.  Compute  the  truth  score  
A  good  starting  formula  is:  

--- PAGE 11 ---
truth_score  =      w1  *  source_reliability    +  w2  *  evidence_specificity    +  w3  *  corroboration_strength    +  w4  *  temporal_consistency    -  w5  *  contradiction_pressure    -  w6  *  manipulation_signal   
With  normalized  weights  such  as:  
w1  =  0.22  w2  =  0.18  w3  =  0.24  w4  =  0.14  w5  =  0.14  w6  =  0.08   
Then  clamp  to  [0,1].  
But  the  better  production-grade  approach  is  to  split  score  into  two  layers:  
Layer  1:  Evidence  Support  Score  
Measures  support  only.  
support_score  =      0.30  *  source_reliability    +  0.25  *  evidence_specificity    +  0.30  *  corroboration_strength    +  0.15  *  temporal_consistency   
Layer  2:  Risk  Penalty  Score  
Measures  reasons  to  distrust  it.  
risk_penalty  =      0.60  *  contradiction_pressure    +  0.40  *  manipulation_signal   
Final  Score  truth_score  =  support_score  *  (1  -  risk_penalty)

Independence  filter:  critical  protection  
One  of  the  biggest  failures  in  truth  systems  is  false  corroboration.  

--- PAGE 14 ---
If  40  articles  all  trace  back  to  the  same  wire  service,  leaked  memo,  or  reposted  claim,  they  must  
not

You  need  an  independence  reducer:  
def  deduplicate_independent_sources(evidence_list):      grouped  =  cluster_by_origin_chain(evidence_list)      return  [select_best_representative(group)  for  group  in  grouped]   
Cluster  by:  
●  same  publisher  family  ●  same  original  quoted  source  ●  same  document  hash  ●  same  wire  article  lineage  ●  same  social  media  origin  chain  
Without  this,  the  engine  becomes  vulnerable  to  manufactured  consensus.  
Contradiction  model  
Not  all  contradictions  are  equal.  
You  should  score  contradiction  pressure  by:  
●  credibility  of  the  contradicting  source  ●  directness  of  contradiction  ●  overlap  in  time  and  context  ●  whether  the  contradiction  addresses  the  same  atomic  claim  ●  whether  the  contradiction  is  merely  absence,  not  conflict  
Suggested  function:  
contradiction_pressure  =      Σ  (credibility  ×  directness  ×  overlap  ×  independence_weight)   
Normalized  to  [0,1].

●  high  contradiction  with  high  stakes  ●  high  manipulation  probability  ●  evidence  is  thin  but  impact  is  large  ●  truth  score  lands  in  a  contested  band  ●  source  provenance  is  weak  ●  public  release  threshold  is  not  met  
A  truth  engine  that  never  says  “review  required”  is  not  trustworthy.  
What  the  core  math  should  actually  do  
The  current  weighted  WEAPR  model  is  a  good  v1  base,  but  for  the  proprietary  engine  you  want  
a
 
stronger
 
architecture.
 
Use  a  two-layer  model.  
Layer  A:  Feature  extraction  
Per  claim,  compute:  
●  evidence_support  

--- PAGE 53 ---
●  source_reliability  ●  chain_of_custody_strength  ●  independence_adjusted_corroboration  ●  temporal_coherence  ●  causal_coherence  ●  contradiction_pressure  ●  narrative_instability  ●  deception_signal  ●  provenance_integrity  ●  revision_stability  ●  domain_expert_alignment where  applicable  
Layer  B:  Bayesian  fusion  
Compute:  
P(claim_supported  |  evidence,  provenance,  contradiction,  temporal_state,  deception_state)   
That  is  a  better  core  than  raw  linear  weighting.  
A  practical  form:  
posterior_truth  =      prior_truth      ×  likelihood(evidence_support)      ×  likelihood(source_reliability)      ×  likelihood(independence_adjusted_corroboration)      ×  likelihood(temporal_coherence)      ×  likelihood(causal_coherence)      ×  penalty(contradiction_pressure)      ×  penalty(deception_signal)      ×  penalty(provenance_integrity_failures)   
Then  normalize.  
This  lets  you  update  beliefs  as  new  evidence  arrives.  That  matters  for  history,  law,  OSINT,  and  
current
 
events.
